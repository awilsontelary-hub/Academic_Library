Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about using our library system

Getting Started

How do I create an account?

Click on the "Join Now" or "Create an account" button on the homepage or login page. Fill out the registration form with your details and select whether you're a Student or Staff member. After submitting, you'll be able to log in immediately.

What's the difference between Student and Staff accounts?

Student accounts can browse books, borrow them, add reviews, and manage their borrowed books. Staff accounts have additional privileges including uploading new books, managing all borrows, viewing detailed statistics, and accessing administrative features.

I forgot my password. How can I reset it?

Currently, password resets must be handled by the library administrator. Please contact the library staff for assistance with password recovery.

Browsing and Searching Books

How do I find books in the library?

You can browse books by clicking "Browse Books" on the homepage or navigation menu. Use the search functionality to find books by title, author, or category. You can also filter books by category using the dropdown filters.

What information is available for each book?

Each book page displays:

  • Title, author, and category
  • Detailed description
  • Availability status
  • Reviews and ratings from other users
  • Download and preview options (if available)

Can I preview books before borrowing them?

Yes! Many books offer preview functionality. Look for the "Preview" button on the book detail page to get a sample of the content before deciding to borrow.

Borrowing Books

How do I borrow a book?

Navigate to the book you want to borrow and click the "Borrow Book" button. Your borrow request will be submitted for approval by library staff. You'll be notified once your request is approved or if additional information is needed.

How long can I keep a borrowed book?

The standard borrowing period is typically 2 weeks, but this may vary depending on the book type and library policies. Check your dashboard for specific due dates for your borrowed books.

How do I return a book?

Go to your student dashboard where you'll see all your currently borrowed books. Click the "Return" button next to the book you want to return. The return will be processed automatically.

Can I download books to read offline?

Yes, for books you have successfully borrowed, you'll see a "Download" option that allows you to save the book file to your device for offline reading.

Account Management

How do I update my profile information?

Click on your username in the top navigation bar and select "Profile" or "Update Profile". You can modify your first name, last name, and email address. Note that usernames cannot be changed after account creation.

Where can I see my borrowing history?

Your complete borrowing history is available on your dashboard. Students can access this through the "Student Dashboard" and staff through the "Teacher Dashboard". This shows current borrows, past returns, and pending requests.

How do I add reviews for books I've read?

After borrowing and reading a book, visit the book's detail page and look for the "Add Review" button. You can rate the book and write a detailed review to help other users discover great content.

For Staff Members

How do I add new books to the library?

Staff members can upload new books through the "Upload Book" option in the Teacher Dashboard. Fill out the book details including title, author, category, description, and upload the book file. The book will be immediately available for students to browse and borrow.

How do I manage student borrow requests?

Use the "Manage Borrows" section in your Teacher Dashboard to see all pending borrow requests. You can approve or reject requests, and view the complete borrowing activity across the library.

Where can I view library statistics?

The "Statistics" section provides comprehensive analytics including total books, active users, download counts, popular books, and borrowing trends. This helps in making informed decisions about library management and collection development.

Technical Issues

I'm having trouble downloading a book. What should I do?

Ensure you have successfully borrowed the book first. Check your internet connection and try again. Some browsers may block downloads, so check your browser's download settings. If the problem persists, contact library staff for assistance.

The website isn't loading properly. What can I do?

Try refreshing the page or clearing your browser cache. Ensure you're using a modern, updated browser. If you continue experiencing issues, try accessing the site from a different device or network. Contact technical support if problems persist.

Which file formats are supported for books?

The library supports various formats including PDF, EPUB, DOCX, and other common document formats. For best compatibility, PDF files are recommended for most academic materials.
